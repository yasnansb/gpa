GPA Calculation Rule — Ilia State University

  1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the products of the Grade Points (GP) earned in each course and the credits obtained for that course, divided by the sum of the credits of all courses the student has taken: GPA = Σ (GP of each course × credits of each course) / Σ (credits of each course)
  2. GP (Grade Point) is the score received in a single course, converted to a 4.00-point scale.
  3. Under the GP system, grades fall into the following categories: F: 0–50 pts (Fail) · E: 51–60 · D: 61–70 · C: 71–80 · B: 81–90 · A: 91–95 · A+: 96–100
  4. Within each course, an E-category grade (51–60) is always equal to 1.00 GP. The D, C, B and A category grades (61 up to and including 95 points) increase evenly, in steps of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. An A+ grade (96–100 points) is always equal to 4.00 GP.
  5. GPA is awarded for all academic programs except doctoral studies, and is not awarded for certificate programs.
  6. Only the final GPA is awarded at Ilia State University, and it is reflected in the diploma supplement (this does not apply to the single-cycle Physician's Diploma program).
  7. When the final GPA is calculated, the result is rounded to the nearest tenth.
